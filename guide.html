Dashboard Overview
View in Dashboard →
The first page you see every morning. It shows your most important numbers from the previous day and week across all locations.
Total Revenue
Gross sales across all locations for the selected period. Use the period selector (Today / Week / Month) to change the view.
Compare to same period last week using the % change indicator
Prime Cost %
Food cost + labor cost combined as a percentage of revenue. This is your single most important number.
Target: under 62% for full service, under 55% for fast casual
Food Cost %
What you spent on ingredients as a percentage of revenue. Updated daily from your POS and inventory data.
Target: 28–34% depending on concept
Labor Cost %
Total wages paid as a percentage of revenue. Includes FOH, BOH, and management.
Target: 28–35% depending on service style
How to read this page

Green means you're within target range. Amber means you're close to the edge. Red means you're over. The target ranges are based on what you entered during setup — adjust them anytime in Settings.

Operations
Cost Breakdown
A full breakdown of every cost category as a percentage of revenue. Use this page to find where money is leaking.
Food Cost
Ingredient spend divided by revenue. If this is trending up, check your portion sizes, waste, and supplier pricing.
Labor Cost
Total wages divided by revenue. High labor on slow nights is the most common fix — compare to Sales by Hour.
Fixed Costs
Rent, utilities, insurance, and software — costs that don't change with volume. Shown as a monthly total.
Prime Cost
Food + labor. The number most operators track daily. Every 1% improvement goes straight to profit.
Industry benchmarks

Full service: food 30–34%, labor 30–35%, prime 62–65%
Fast casual: food 28–32%, labor 25–30%, prime 55–60%
Fine dining: food 28–35%, labor 30–40%, prime 60–68%

Overview
Daily Sales
Revenue charted day by day. Use this to spot patterns — which days consistently underperform, and whether you're trending up or down week over week.
Daily Revenue
Gross sales for each day. Click any bar to see a breakdown by location.
Cover Count
Number of guests served. Compare with revenue to track average check trends.
Average Check
Revenue divided by covers. A falling average check can mean fewer add-ons or a menu mix shift.
Week-over-Week
How this week compares to the same days last week. The most useful comparison for restaurants.
What to look for

If one day consistently underperforms, consider a prix fixe or happy hour promotion for that day. If your average check is falling, look at whether your team is suggesting add-ons and specials.

Sales by Hour
Revenue broken down by hour of the day. The most actionable page in the dashboard — it tells you exactly when to schedule staff and when to cut.
Revenue by Hour
How much you made each hour. Peaks tell you your busiest windows. Valleys tell you when you're overstaffed.
Covers by Hour
Number of guests per hour. Use this to determine FOH staffing ratios (1 server per 4–6 covers is typical).
Staffing Guide
Suggested FOH and BOH headcount by hour based on your historical volume. Adjust in Settings.
Revenue per Labor Hour
How much revenue each labor hour generated. Lower numbers mean you were overstaffed for that window.
How to use this in practice

Print the staffing guide and give it to your floor manager every Monday. If revenue per labor hour is consistently low between 2pm–5pm, consider cutting that shift or running it leaner.

Labor & Scheduling
Actual hours worked versus scheduled hours, broken down by employee and role. Overtime alerts fire automatically when someone goes over 40 hours.
Scheduled vs Actual
How many hours were scheduled vs how many were actually worked. Large gaps mean your scheduling needs tightening.
Overtime Alert
Flags any employee approaching or over 40 hours for the week. Acts early enough to adjust the schedule.
Overtime costs 1.5× — catching it Wednesday saves real money

Labor Cost per Cover

Tip

Check this page every Wednesday. If anyone is already at 32+ hours and you have a busy weekend ahead, you can adjust the schedule before overtime kicks in.

Delivery & Online Orders
What you actually made from DoorDash, Uber Eats, and Grubhub — after their commission comes out. Most operators are surprised how little they net.
Gross Revenue
What customers paid on the platform before commission.
Commission
What the platform kept. DoorDash typically 20–30%, Uber Eats 15–30%, Grubhub 15–25%.
Net Revenue
What actually hit your account after commission. This is the number that matters.
If net margin on delivery orders is under 10%, delivery may be losing you money
By Platform
Side-by-side comparison of each platform's volume and net revenue. Use this to decide where to focus.
Important

Delivery orders have higher food costs (larger portions, packaging) and the same labor costs as dine-in, but you're giving up 20–30% to the platform. A $40 delivery order might net you $28 — less than the same order dine-in at $40 with no commission.

Break-Even Calculator
How many covers you need to serve before you start making money. Share this number with your floor manager every morning.
Break-Even Covers
The number of guests you need to cover your fixed costs and variable costs for the day. Every cover after this is profit.
Break-Even Revenue
The revenue number you need to hit before you start profiting. Calculated using your fixed costs and prime cost %.
Today's Pace
Based on your Sales by Hour data, whether you're on track to hit break-even by close.
Contribution Margin
How much each cover contributes toward covering your fixed costs after variable costs are subtracted.
How to use this

Every morning, tell your floor manager: "We need X covers to break even today." It reframes the shift as a concrete target rather than just showing up and hoping for a good night.

Voids & Comps
Every voided transaction and comp logged automatically from your POS. High void rates are often a sign of theft, training issues, or system problems.
Void Rate
Voids as a percentage of total transactions. Industry average is 1–2%. Above 4% warrants investigation.
Alert fires automatically when rate exceeds your threshold
Comp Rate
Comps as a percentage of revenue. Some comps are intentional (manager discretion), others aren't.
Target: under 1% of revenue
By Employee
Which employees are voiding or comping the most. An outlier here is a red flag.
By Item
Which menu items are voided most. High voids on one item can mean a training issue or a bad item.
What to look for

A server who voids 3× more than colleagues, voids that happen after payment (possible theft), or a sudden spike on a particular item. PrimeIQ alerts you automatically — you just need to follow up.

P&L Statement
Your full income statement — revenue, cost of goods, labor, fixed costs, and net profit — formatted the way your accountant expects it.
Gross Revenue
Total sales before any deductions.
Cost of Goods Sold
Food and beverage cost for the period.
Gross Profit
Revenue minus food cost. This covers labor and fixed costs.
Net Operating Profit
What's left after food, labor, and fixed costs. This is what you actually made.
Export for your accountant

Use the Export button to download your P&L as a CSV. It's formatted to match standard restaurant P&L templates — your accountant can import it directly.
