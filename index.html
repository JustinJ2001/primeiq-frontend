The problems every independent operator has

If you run 2–5 locations, you've felt all of these

📅
You find out you had a bad week on Friday.
By then it's too late to fix it. You could have caught it Tuesday and adjusted staffing on Wednesday.
📊
Toast shows you sales. It doesn't show you profit.
Your POS has no idea what you paid in rent, what DoorDash kept, or whether Tuesday was actually profitable.
🛵
DoorDash takes 22%. How much was that last week in dollars?
After commissions, packaging, and food cost, your delivery margin is a lot thinner than it looks. Most operators can't answer this.
💼
Your accountant tells you how last month went. Last month.
You need visibility daily, not 30 days later. Problems fixed Tuesday cost far less than problems discovered at month end.
🧾
Voids and comps add up quietly.
A few hundred dollars a week in voids is $15,000 a year. Most operators have no idea what their number actually is.
👥
Labor costs creep up without you noticing.
Someone picks up an extra shift, someone hits overtime. By the time you see it in payroll, the week is already over.
